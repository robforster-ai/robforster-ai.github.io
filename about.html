Enterprise AI Transformation Lead

Rob Forster

I have spent more than 20 years helping large organisations get complex technology programmes over the line — from ERP transformations to digital operating model redesigns. For the last several years, that work has been entirely focused on enterprise AI: how organisations adopt it, scale it, and make it stick.

My background is in delivery, not research. I have led and overseen multi-million pound AI programmes for some of the UK and Europe’s largest organisations, working across financial services, retail, public sector, telecommunications, and energy. Much of that work has been through IBM Consulting, where I have built and run practices focused on applied AI and intelligent process transformation.

What I write about comes directly from that experience: the patterns that appear when you try to scale AI beyond a pilot, the organisational dynamics that derail technically sound programmes, and the programme management disciplines that separate successful transformations from expensive experiments. I am not interested in the hype cycle — I am interested in what actually works.

Outside of AI delivery, I think a lot about knowledge management, operating model design, and how organisations build the capability to keep improving after the consultants leave. If any of that resonates, I’d welcome the conversation.

Focus Areas

  • AI programme delivery & governance
  • Knowledge management & institutional intelligence
  • AI platform implementation & adoption
  • Operating model design for AI at scale
